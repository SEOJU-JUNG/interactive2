About Bitcoin
Bitcoin is a type of crypto currency and it explains its currency mechanism. But those currency cannot be physically hold by our hands. It is just a code which floats on online. Crypto currency, Bitcoin does not have an issuer since bitcoin is not run by specified individuals or companies. They use P2P mechanism which spreads their system into multiple user’s computer. For the micropayment or transfer of funds, its popularity is rapidly increasing, due to easy account making.

Electricity consumption

The increase in power consumption due to Bitcoin became a controversial subject. With the current trend of electricity usage increment, by July 2019 it will exceed USA’s entire electricity consumption.
Eric Holthaus, a meteorologist who intensively declares this problem, in December 2017, at the news website called ‘Grist’ he mentioned that by the year 2020 February, the electricity usage of Bitcoin would reach the same amount of the global power consumption.
Holthaus said that the majority of Bitcoin mining is currently produced in China, whereby China uses 72% of its electricity generation from coal power plants. In other words, they are burning massive amounts of coal to mine Bitcoin. Ant pool(22.4%), BTC.com(17.1%) whereby all of the monopolized Bitcoin firms are from China. Digital currency website ‘Digiconomist.’
Estimated 2,31.6 million ton of CO2 annual production due to Bitcoin. According to Digiconomist’s Bitcoin Energy Consumption Index, on February 5th, 2018, 47.26TW amount of electricity is used for Bitcoin mining, which is the same amount of USA’s 43.7 million households power usage.
