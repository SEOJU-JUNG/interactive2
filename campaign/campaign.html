Our mission

We call for the government to tighten disposable plastic legislation
and for companies to declare a reduction in plastic consumption.
Please agree to the The National Petition of South Korea!

Giant Plastic island
floating in the
Pacific Ocean?

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch (GPGP) is the largest of the five offshore plastic accumulation zones in the world’s oceans. The GPGP covers an estimated surface area of 1.6 million square kilometers, an area seven times the size of S.Korea. It is located between East-Asia countries and the USA, which eventually return back and harmfully impacting on us health.
To against for further damage, many countries, including China, the USA, and Japan, have been intensifying the plastic laws. Korea also should take responsibility for the issue and tighten plastic laws up to the level of other countries who have been practically implementing such laws. Otherwise, it will impact our ecosystems, health, and economies.

OUR 3 legal requirements

PACKAGEING

Intensify laws on plastic packaging; Forbid the provision of disposable products for free.

CARE PRODUCTS

STYROFOAM

fish farms and provide free
eco-friendly materials instead.

S.Korea is one of the world's biggest plastic consumers.

In 2015, it consumed 317 pounds (144 kilograms) of plastic per capita, surpassing both the US and China at 218 and 138 pounds (99kg and 63 kg), respectively, according to Euromap, an association for plastics and rubber machinery manufacturers.

While South Korea last year banned single-use plastic bags from supermarkets, Greenpeace's Kim says more must be done. " Consumers can bear the discomfort and reduce their use of single-use cups and straws," she says. " But a greater portion of plastic production and waste is created by industry and their packaging. It's time for industry to reduce plastic.”
